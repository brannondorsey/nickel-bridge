4Marks from the real Boulevard "Nickel" Bridge — deck on top, truss below
What the real bridge looks like (and why it fixes turn 1–2)
It's a deck truss, not an arch: the flat roadway rides on top, deep steel trusses hang underneath, and each continuous span's bottom chord sweeps up in a shallow arch between concrete piers — a Pratt/Warren hybrid with little "mini-trusses" inside each panel. So the ownable silhouette is flat line on top, scalloped structure below — the inverse of every generic bridge logo (and of my old arches). 4a evolves 1c to the real geometry; 4d is the calm answer to 1b.
